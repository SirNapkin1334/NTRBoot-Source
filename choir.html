Overview

Soprano is the voice part of a choir that sings the highest. It is typically sung by females, however occasionally some males are able to sing it. This is typically called falsetto. Soprano is defined as singing between 261 and 880 Hz.

Etymology

The word "Soprano" comes from the Italian word sopra, meaning "above," since it is the highest pitch of voice.

Soprano in German is Sopran, and Italian & French both are simply "Soprano."

Styles

Besides Choral music, there are many different styles for Soprano singing. One you might not think of is Operatic Rock, signing rock music with a high, Opera-like pitch.
